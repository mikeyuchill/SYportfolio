Project Overview

Bìxié (辟邪; bìxié; pi-hsieh; lit. "to ward off evil spirits") are female Pixiu (貔貅; píxiū; P'i-hsiu) that ward off evil spirits and help people who are suffering from bad feng shui. They are mythical hybrid creatures that have the head of a Chinese dragon, the body of a lion, and two antlers. Bìxié use their large fangs to protect against demons and diseases, turning their evil essence into wealth. These creatures roam around, guarding their owners from all dangers even through multiple reincarnations.

Bìxié is a 2-player cooperative sidescrolling beat-em-up focused on narrative and theme. The primary gameplay involves solving simple puzzles, fighting enemies, and discovering more about the world through visual novel scenes.

My Contributions to Bìxié

As one of the main programmers and the sole concept designer, I contributed to Bìxié in the following ways:

• Programming & Systems Development:

  • • Developed core gameplay features, including the dialogue system, multiplayer functionality, and puzzle mechanics.
  • • Iterated on combat system mechanics, improving responsiveness and balancing internal gameplay elements.
  • • Implemented an internal economy system to enhance in-game progression and maintain engagement.
  • • Designed and optimized the character controller for a fluid and dynamic movement system.

• Game Concept & Design:

  • • Created the game’s concept, story background, and core mechanics, establishing the unique identity of Bìxié.
  • • Designed the gameplay flow to align with the story and puzzle-solving mechanics.

• Playtesting & Iteration:

  • • Organized and led playtesting sessions (both in-house and external), collecting feedback and refining gameplay.
  • • Analyzed and implemented player feedback, making weekly iterations to enhance user experience.
  • • Recorded gameplay footage for documentation and future iteration planning.
